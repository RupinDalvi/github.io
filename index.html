Projects

1. Cyclist Power, Gear & Speed Tracker

Overview:

  • A responsive web app that tracks and analyzes cycling power, gear and speed in real-time using GPS or simulation, elevation data, and live wind information.
  • Includes an “Auto Pose” mode to adjust drag area (CdA) automatically based on terrain gradient.

Features:

  • Samples location, cadence (needs cadence sensor), elevation, and wind every 5 seconds for high-resolution hill detection.
  • Physics-based power calculation accounting for gravity, rolling resistance, and aerodynamic drag.
  • Auto Pose option: out-of-saddle (CdA 0.40 m²) on climbs, hoods (0.32 m²) on flats, drops (0.28 m²) on descents.
  • Live display of recent power, speed, gradient, wind, and current CdA.
  • End-of-session summary: average power & speed, calories burned, and total elevation gain.
  • Downloadable CSV of timestamp, GPS coords, altitude, power, speed, CdA, and gradient.

3. Cycling Cadence Conformer (Impractical but fun project)

Overview:

  • A fun but utterly impractical experiment. It does seem to work fairly well, but it requires you to strap the phone to your knee or keep it in your bib pocket while riding to get the cadence making it really awkward to use in practice.
  • A browser-based prototype that integrates a YouTube video player with real-time cadence feedback via the device’s accelerometer.
  • Adjusts audio volume and emits noise cues when your pedaling RPM deviates from a set target.

Features:

  • Load and play any YouTube video in-page.
  • Set a desired cadence (RPM) target.
  • Measure pedaling cadence using the device motion sensor.
  • Smooth noisy sensor data with EMA filters and amplitude thresholding.
  • Provide audio feedback by lowering video volume or emitting noise cues.
  • Responsive, mobile-friendly UI with no external dependencies.
